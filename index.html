Abstract

State‑of‑the‑art scRNA‑seq datasets routinely exceed 105 cells, rendering classical dimensionality‑reduction tools inadequate for preserving both global and local transcriptomic geometry. AGW‑Embed couples Nyström‑landmark Sinkhorn distances with a graph‑Wasserstein regularizer, achieving globally faithful, locally coherent embeddings in quasi‑linear time and memory.

Method at a Glance

  • Dual‑scale objective: global Nyström Sinkhorn OT + local Graph‑Wasserstein.
  • Quasi‑linear complexity: Õ(n3/2g) time, O(n) memory.
  • GPU‑friendly: < 1 GB memory for 3 k cells; 10× faster than full‑matrix OT.
  • Biologically informed cost: incorporates GO/PPI gene relationships.

Quantitative Benchmarks

AGW‑Embed attains the highest global distance correlation (0.699) and spectral‑graph concordance (0.095), outperforming scGNN by 24% and 20%, respectively, while matching or exceeding classical and deep models on Earth‑Mover's Distance and k‑NN preservation.
